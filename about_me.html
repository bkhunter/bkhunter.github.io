About Me

Hello! I’m Ben Hunter - thank you for visiting my little corner of the web.

I’m a software developer who recently graduated with a B.Sc. in Computing Science and a Certificate in Computer Game Development from the University of Alberta. I love to make software, design and create things, play music, and do anything that allows me to be creative.


Education

Although I really enjoy the tree of computing science and all of its branches, I didn’t always know it was for me. In fact, for the first two years of my undergraduate career, I was enrolled in the general science program with a biology major and a chemistry minor (I had been watching a lot of "House" and thought medicine might be for me). However, the classes I took were not indicative of a career in medicine; Latin, History, Psychology, Geology, Philosophy and other extraneous courses were among my general science course-load. In my second semester of second year I took CMPUT 101 on a whim, and absolutely loved it; it was the first class where I actually wanted to explore the material in my free time. While I didn’t know exactly what I wanted with a degree in computing, I felt it would be a good choice for my future. Fast forward three years and I have a broad skill set in the discipline including programming, agile development, networks, databases, web applications, video game development, 3D animation and graphics, algorithms, and more.

Speaking of Video Games...

One of the most exciting applications of computing for me is video games. The possibility that something I imagine or dream about could be put into an interactive format to provoke emotion or enjoyment in another person, is something I just find so beautiful. I think I’m drawn to games because I think of them as the accumulation of many different skills, ideas, and experiences. I feel that matches my strengths as I’m interested in a lot of different things and prefer a broad skill set to a singular specialty. As mentioned earlier, I graduated with a certificate in computer game development, which enabled me to take game-focused courses and allowed me the opportunity to create several games with teams during my degree. I’m still not sure what the future has in store for me, but I intend on ensuring games are in it, even if as a hobby.

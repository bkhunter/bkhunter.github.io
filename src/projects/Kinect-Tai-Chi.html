Kinect Tai Chi

September - December 2015

The task? Use the Microsoft Kinect with the Unity game engine to create an application to help children diagnosed with developmental disorders learn Tai Chi.

Overview

This project was developed with researchers at the University of Alberta and the University of Sapporo. The objective was to assist players in learning Tai Chi, a traditional Chinese martial art involving a series of movements performed in a slow, focused manner. The hypothesis was that the slow/methodical movement of Tai Chi would help developmentally challenged children with physical, social, and cognitive development.

A Microsoft Kinect was used to both record and analyze a persons movement, while the actual game was built with the Unity game engine. I especially enjoyed working with Unity, as it allowed us to introduce game-like features to keep the application engaging. It was a great experience to work on what is known in the industry as a ‘serious game’, a game that is entertaining but more importantly has potential to make an impact in the real world. This project was one of the more significant of my undergraduate degree and gave me the opportunity to gain experience with agile processes, game development, and real client interaction.

The Paris Site

January - April 2015

The final project for a 3D graphics and animation course at the University of Alberta with the guidelines of ‘Paris Tourism’. Due to strong creative forces and unfortunate group dynamics the result was a great learning experience, but a very strange video.

Overview

In the beginning, the general guidelines of the project were to make a tourism video about Paris, however groups could deviate if they wanted, with instructor approval. One member of my group had the interesting idea of creating a story around 3 characters, and re-telling it from each of their perspectives. This was especially cool, since we could very easily change the background settings for each perspective (i.e. make it gloomy and rainy for one, sunny and bright for another), and give us extra ability to implicitly describe the characters. The premise was that aliens have landed at the eiffel tower and are using their technology to gain insight into human experience, hence the name The Paris Site (pun intentional). We used 3Ds Max to animate and create all the scenes and characters, while sound and post production was all done by the project lead. My contribution to this project was two fully modelled and rigged characters and some monsters (pictured below), as well as several animated scenes, and some character voices.

While The Paris Site began significantly different from what was expected, it only continued in this direction over the term. This was largely due to one of the team members, and their lack of contribution and helpful attitude. Because of this, the workload increased for all other members of the team, quality dropped, and cuts had to be made to the story which would have helped give it meaning and sense. The really important lessons I took away from making this video came from dealing with this less than optimal work environment, and from the vast amount of time it took to make this thing. Make no mistake, creating The Paris Site was nothing short of an ordeal, and gave me the utmost respect for beautiful 3d models, and their overworked creators. If I had to try to describe it, I would say The Paris Site is a postmodern take on love, perspective, and aliens, set in Paris, France. However I prefer saying that it’s up to the viewer’s interpretation. Enjoy the video below, just remember to keep an open mind.
